Welcome to the Texas Disaster Volunteer Registry



Thank you for your interest in volunteering your time, talents, and experiences to assist with medical and healthcare response to disasters.

The Texas Disaster Volunteer Registry Web site, maintained by the Department of State Health Services (DSHS), was created to identify and link volunteers to those in our Texas communities who need medical help during or after a disaster.  The Volunteer Registry provides a secure database with readily available, verifiable information regarding each volunteer's identity, qualifications, training and experience.  The Registry helps provide a timely, effective response to disasters and ensures the appropriate match of a volunteer's skills and abilities with the needs of the emergency situation.

As a prospective volunteer, please keep in mind the following:

  • Your information will be kept secure and shared only with emergency management officials during a disaster response.
  • As a volunteer, you have the right to say "yes" or "no" to any requests for your services.
  • As a volunteer, you have the right to decide how far you are willing to travel and how long you are willing to serve.
  • When your services are requested for disaster response, you will be given as many details as possible about your role and responsibilities as well as situational or "Just in Time" training.

Together as Texans, we can stand ready to respond proudly and effectively to disasters that threaten our State.  Thank you again for your interest in volunteering -- and for standing with us -- in our response to disasters and public health emergencies.

Before you register, please go to the Help button (upper right) for more information.
